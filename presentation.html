This presentation is on the history of the music streaming bar. Presented by Jamie Han.


1980s

The advent of computer operating systems: Macintosh's classic operating system was first released in 1984, called "System 1". Microsoft released "Windows 1.0" in November 1985, which was the first version of Microsoft Windows series. Basic designs indicating certain function and icons were made in these earlier stages.

Macintosh OS Microsoft Windows

The design of Mac OS informs much of how we think of mouse cursors today. Their limited resolution, effected their aesthetic representation.

Early 1990s

Two operating systems' iconic media players were made in this period. Quicktime player and Windows Media Player(WMP) of this time show basic and flat design. They have standard media control icons such as play ( Octicons-playback-play.svg ), pause ( Octicons-playback-pause.svg ), fastforward, backforward, and stop ( Octicons-primitive-square.svg ) buttons.

Mid 1990s

More music player softwares release. Apple took over SoundJam MP and opened iTunes. Famous media player Winamp was released in 1997 and was popularly used as the sole option for playing MP3-compressed music on Microsoft Windows. Initial designs were not very different from those of early 1990s, but as versions were being updated, the design became more complex and it offered 'skins' to alter the aesthetic design of the player.
