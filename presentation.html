History of Media Player

Visual history of Media Streaming Players

This presentation is on the history of the music player and its streaming bar. Presented by Jamie Han.



1980s

The advent of computer operating systems: Macintosh's classic operating system was first released in 1984, called "System 1".
Microsoft released "Windows 1.0" in November 1985, which was the first version of Microsoft Windows series.
Basic designs indicating certain function and icons were made in these earlier stages, but media player wasn't publicized yet.
Some aspects of fundamental design to be developed can be found from volume bars and icons.

Early 1990s

Two operating systems' iconic media players were made in this period. Quicktime player and Windows Media Player(WMP) of this time show basic and flat design.
They have standard media control icons such as play, pause, fastforward, backforward, and stop buttons. Still has pixelated aspect, mostly in grayscale.

Mid 1990s~Early 2000s

More music player softwares release. Apple took over SoundJam MP and opened iTunes.
Also, famous media player Winamp was released in 1997 and was popularly used as the sole option for playing MP3-compressed music on Microsoft Windows.
Initial designs were not very different from those of early 1990s, remaining in gray rectangular boxes, but icons had more color and dimensionality than before.
Customized media player softwares for vernacular, amateuristic and personal websites were more widely developed and distributed by the users.
People enjoyed 'creating own skins' that could be applied to show off their individuality.

2000s

P2P websites' popularity provoked further developments in media softwares, while music became more widely sharable and a lot of music streaming services started during this area.
In US, streaming service Spotify started in 2006. In South Korea, most P2P file sharing websites turned into legal music streaming services;
meanwhile, the majority of them prevailed in the market in concert with wireless carriers. This eventually lead to the development of moblie streaming applications as well.
Continuing from trend in early 2000s, designs of icons, buttons, bars and equalizers became more complex with a sense of dimension with effects such as drop shadow, emboss and luster.

2010s~today

Importance of mobile UI/UX has grown more than ever, and existing OS systems continuously being refined.
Major design change occurs: 3-Dimensional trend declines while much more minimal and flat style of design prevails; designers abandon trying to make'realistic and detailed' icons.
Streaming bars and icons are substituted by thin single stroke, icons get rid of emboss and gradient.
Apple music started its service, and Microsoft newly launced media player called Groove Music instead of classic WMP in their new OS, Windows10.
